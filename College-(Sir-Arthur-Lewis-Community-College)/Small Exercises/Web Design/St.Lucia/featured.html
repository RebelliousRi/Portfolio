Featured Tourist Sites In Saint Lucia

Discover some of the most iconic and beautiful locations in Saint Lucia. From historical landmarks to natural wonders, these sites will captivate your imagination and leave you with unforgettable memories.

The Pitons

The Pitons are two volcanic spires located on the southwest coast of Saint Lucia. Gros Piton and Petit Piton are iconic symbols of the island and are part of a UNESCO World Heritage site. The Pitons are popular for hiking, with Gros Piton offering a more accessible trail, while Petit Piton presents a more challenging climb. The surrounding area also offers breathtaking views, snorkeling, and boat tours. Whether you're an adventurer or simply want to admire their beauty from afar, the Pitons are a must-see.

Pigeon Island National Park

Pigeon Island National Park is a historic site that offers a glimpse into Saint Lucia's past. Once a strategic military base, the island is now connected to the mainland by a causeway. Visitors can explore the ruins of military buildings, hike up to Fort Rodney for panoramic views, and enjoy the serene beaches. Pigeon Island is also a popular spot for picnics, cultural festivals, and enjoying the natural beauty of Saint Lucia. It is an ideal destination for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Diamond Falls Botanical Gardens

The Diamond Falls Botanical Gardens is one of the most beautiful and tranquil attractions in Saint Lucia. Located in Soufrière, the gardens are home to a wide variety of tropical plants and flowers. The centerpiece of the gardens is the Diamond Waterfall, which is fed by sulphur springs and changes colors due to mineral deposits. The gardens also feature mineral baths that were originally built for King Louis XVI of France. It's a perfect place to relax, take a leisurely stroll, and immerse yourself in the island's natural beauty.

Sulphur Springs Park

Sulphur Springs Park, known as the Caribbean's only drive-in volcano, is located near the town of Soufrière. The park offers a unique experience where visitors can drive up to the crater of the dormant volcano and witness the bubbling sulphur springs. The area is also known for its mud baths, which are said to have therapeutic properties. The hot springs and mud baths are a popular attraction for both tourists and locals looking to relax and rejuvenate. Guided tours are available to explain the history and geology of the area.

Marigot Bay

Marigot Bay is often described as one of the most beautiful bays in the Caribbean. Known for its picturesque setting, the bay is surrounded by lush green hills and dotted with yachts and sailboats. It has been used as a filming location for several movies due to its scenic beauty. Visitors can enjoy boat tours, kayaking, or simply relax at one of the waterfront cafes. Marigot Bay is an ideal spot for those looking to experience the serene and tranquil side of Saint Lucia.
